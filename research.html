Research

Thermal Decomposition of Energetic Materials

This research focuses on how surface morphology influences the low-temperature sublimation and decomposition of ammonium perchlorate crystals. By combining atomistic simulations and reaction pathway analysis, the goal is to understand how flat surfaces, steps, kinks, corners, and other defects modify molecular detachment, proton transfer, and the early stages of thermal degradation.

Stress Corrosion Cracking

This work investigates how water interacts with strained metal-oxide surfaces and crack-like environments, revealing atomistic mechanisms of bond breaking, dissolution, and chemically assisted degradation. These simulations help connect local chemical reactions at oxide surfaces to stress corrosion cracking and long-term materials failure.

  • L. C. Felix, Q.-K. Li, E. S. Penev and B. I. Yakobson, Materials, 18 (3), 538 (2025).

Thermal Transport at Nanoscale

This research explores how nanoscale geometry and topology can control heat flow in low-dimensional materials. In knotted graphene nanoribbons, local distortions introduce thermal resistance, producing temperature jumps similar to Kapitza resistance and allowing heat transport to be tuned by mechanical and structural design.

  • L. C. Felix, A. F. Fonseca and D. S. Galvao, arXiv, 2208.01751 (2022).

Mechanical Response of Carbon Nanomaterials

This research line focuses on the mechanical behavior of carbon-based nanomaterials and architected carbon structures. Using density functional theory, molecular dynamics, and multiscale modeling, these studies examined how topology, porosity, defects, and structural design affect deformation, fracture, impact resistance, energy absorption, and thermal stability.

  • L. C. Felix, C. F. Woellner and D. S. Galvao, Carbon, 157, 670–680 (2020).
